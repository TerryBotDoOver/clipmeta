# How to Keyword Drone Footage for Maximum Sales

In the competitive world of stock footage, proper keywording can make or break the visibility and sales of your drone footage. With the increasing popularity of aerial videos, understanding how to effectively keyword your content is crucial. This post will guide you through the specific keywording strategies for drone and aerial footage that buyers actually search for.

## Why Keywording Matters in Drone Footage

Keywording is the process of adding descriptive tags to your footage. This helps potential buyers find your content during their search. With well-chosen keywords, your drone footage can stand out and become more discoverable. In short, effective keywording increases the chances of your footage being purchased.

## How to Choose the Right Keywords for Drone Footage

Choosing the right keywords for your drone footage involves understanding what buyers are searching for. Here's how you can do it:

1. **Identify Your Footage's Unique Attributes**:
   - *Location*: Is your footage capturing a famous landmark or a lesser-known place?
   - *Time of Day*: Is it a sunrise, sunset, or midday capture?
   - *Weather Conditions*: Is it clear, cloudy, or stormy?
   - *Activities*: Are there any specific activities or events happening in the footage?

2. **Use Descriptive and Specific Language**:
   - Avoid generic terms like "drone video" unless it's absolutely necessary.
   - Instead, use specific terms that describe the scene, such as "aerial view of New York City skyline at sunset".

3. **Consider the Buyer’s Perspective**:
   - Think about who would want to use your footage and for what purpose.
   - Consider industries like tourism, real estate, and media which frequently use drone footage.

4. **Research Popular and Trending Keywords**:
   - Use tools like Google Trends and keyword planners to discover what is currently in demand.

## Effective Keyword Strategies for Aerial Video Metadata

Understanding how to structure your metadata is just as important as choosing the right keywords. Here are some strategies to optimize your aerial video metadata:

### Prioritize Primary Keywords

Primary keywords should be the ones that best describe the most important aspects of your footage. Place these keywords at the beginning of your metadata. For example, if your footage is an aerial shot of a bustling city during the day, your primary keywords could include "urban aerial view", "cityscape", and "daytime".

### Use Secondary Keywords Wisely

Secondary keywords provide additional context and should complement your primary keywords. These might include the weather conditions, specific landmarks, or events depicted in the footage.

### Avoid Keyword Stuffing

It might be tempting to load your metadata with as many keywords as possible, but this can actually harm your search visibility. Instead, focus on quality over quantity. Ensure that each keyword is relevant and accurately describes the footage.

### Regularly Update Your Keywords

The world of stock footage is dynamic, and trends can change rapidly. Regularly revisiting and updating your keywords ensures that your footage remains relevant and discoverable. ClipMeta makes it easy to manage and update your keywords, ensuring your metadata is always optimized.

## Common Mistakes When Keywording Drone Stock Footage

Even with a solid strategy, there are common pitfalls to avoid when keywording your drone footage:

- **Overusing Generic Terms**: Avoid relying heavily on terms like "drone" and "footage" unless they add value.
- **Ignoring Buyer Behavior**: Failing to consider what buyers are searching for can lead to ineffective keywording.
- **Neglecting Metadata Updates**: As trends evolve, so should your metadata. Regular updates keep your footage competitive.
