## What is Buyer Search Intent?

Buyer search intent refers to the purpose behind a buyer's search query. When someone searches for stock footage, they usually have a specific goal in mind, whether it's to find a particular type of clip, a specific subject matter, or footage that conveys a certain emotion. Understanding and predicting this intent can help you create more targeted and effective keywords.

### Types of Buyer Search Intent

1. **Informational Intent**: Buyers are looking for information about a specific topic or subject.
2. **Navigational Intent**: Buyers want to find a particular website or brand.
3. **Transactional Intent**: Buyers are ready to purchase and are looking for specific products or services.
4. **Commercial Investigation Intent**: Buyers are researching products or services before making a purchase decision.

For stock footage contributors, transactional and informational intents are particularly important. Buyers often search with the intent to find clips that fit their project needs or to gather ideas for future purchases.

## How to Identify Buyer Search Intent

To effectively tailor your keywords, you need to identify the search intent of your potential buyers. Here are some strategies:

- **Analyze Search Queries**: Look at the search terms that lead buyers to your clips. Tools like Google Analytics can provide insights into these queries.
- **Study Market Trends**: Keep an eye on industry trends to understand what kinds of footage are in demand.
- **Engage with Buyers**: If possible, communicate with buyers to get feedback on what they are looking for in stock footage.

## Creating Effective Stock Footage Keywords

Once you understand buyer search intent, you can start crafting keywords that align with their needs. Here are some tips:

### Use Descriptive and Specific Keywords

- **Be Precise**: Include specific details about the clip, such as location, time of day, and subject matter.
- **Use Action-Oriented Words**: Words that describe what is happening in the clip can be very effective.
  
### Incorporate Emotional Keywords

- **Convey Emotions**: Think about the emotions your footage evokes and use related keywords.
  
### Avoid Overstuffing Keywords

- **Keep it Natural**: Your keywords should flow naturally and not feel forced or repetitive.
