Are you ready to upload your stock footage? Before you hit that upload button, ensure your metadata is in top shape. Properly reviewing your stock footage metadata is crucial for enhancing your video's discoverability and appeal to potential buyers. In this guide, we'll walk you through a comprehensive checklist to ensure your uploads are clean and optimized for better search visibility.

## Why Metadata Matters for Stock Footage

Metadata is the backbone of your stock footage. It includes all the descriptive information that helps buyers find your content. Without proper metadata, your clips may get lost in the vast sea of stock footage available online. Efficient metadata can significantly improve your footage's chances of being discovered and purchased.

## The Metadata Review Checklist

### 1. Review File Names

Before uploading, check your file names. They should be descriptive and include relevant keywords. Avoid generic names like "clip001" which do nothing for your search visibility.

- Use clear and descriptive names
- Include keywords relevant to the scene
- Avoid using spaces or special characters

### 2. Optimize Titles

Titles are one of the first things a potential buyer sees. They should be concise yet informative.

- Keep it under 70 characters
- Include the most important keywords
- Describe the content accurately

### 3. Crafting Effective Descriptions

Descriptions give more context to your footage. They should be detailed enough to give buyers a clear idea of what the clip contains.

- Aim for 2-3 sentences
- Include context, such as location or action
- Use natural language and relevant keywords

### 4. Keyword Selection and Placement

Keywords are critical for search engines and stock footage platforms. They help categorize your footage and improve searchability.

#### Primary Keywords

- Choose 5-10 primary keywords that directly describe your clip
- Think about what terms buyers might use to find footage like yours

#### Secondary Keywords

- Add 10-20 secondary keywords for broader search terms
- Consider synonyms and related concepts

### 5. Tagging for Success

Tags are another way to categorize your footage. They should be specific and relevant.

- Use between 5-15 tags
- Include tags that reflect the mood, setting, and subjects of your footage

### 6. Reviewing Technical Metadata

Technical metadata includes information like resolution, frame rate, and codec. Ensure these details are accurate because buyers often filter searches based on technical requirements.

- Check resolution (e.g., 4K, HD)
- Verify frame rate (e.g., 24fps, 30fps)
- Confirm codec (e.g., H.264, ProRes)

### 7. Legal and Release Information

Make sure to include any necessary legal information. This includes model and property releases if applicable.

- Verify all necessary releases are attached
- Double-check that all legal info is correct

### 8. Double-Checking for Errors

Before you upload, take one last look at your metadata for any errors or inconsistencies.

- Check for spelling and grammar mistakes
- Ensure consistency across all metadata fields
- Verify that all information is accurate
