## Understanding Stock Footage Metadata

Metadata is the backbone of your stock footage library. It helps potential buyers find your clips through search engines and stock footage platforms. Well-optimized metadata not only improves visibility but also enhances the chances of your clips being selected. Keywords are a significant part of this metadata, and choosing between single words and phrases can impact your conversion rates dramatically.

### What Are Stock Footage Keywords?

Stock footage keywords are terms or phrases that describe your clips. They help in categorizing and indexing your content so that it can be easily found by users searching for specific themes or subjects. Keywords can be simple single words like "sunset" or "dog", or they can be multi-word phrases like "sunset over ocean" or "golden retriever playing".

## Single Words vs Phrases

Both single-word keywords and phrases have their place in stock footage metadata. Let's break down the benefits and limitations of each.

### Benefits of Single-Word Keywords

- **Broad Reach**: Single words can capture a wide array of searches. For example, "cat" might attract viewers interested in various cat-related content.
- **Simplicity**: They are easy to think of and apply, making the initial tagging process quicker.
- **Flexibility**: They can fit into multiple contexts, making them versatile.

### Limitations of Single-Word Keywords

- **Lack of Specificity**: Single words might not capture specific nuances of your footage.
- **Increased Competition**: More generic terms mean you'll be competing with a larger pool of similar content.

### Benefits of Phrase Keywords

- **Specificity**: Phrases can target niche audiences, like "golden retriever playing fetch", attracting buyers with specific needs.
- **Higher Conversion Rates**: Specific phrases may lead to better conversion rates as they align closely with buyer intent.
- **Reduced Competition**: Less competition for specific phrases means your clips are more likely to stand out.

### Limitations of Phrase Keywords

- **Narrow Reach**: Phrases might not attract as broad an audience as single words.
- **Complexity**: Creating effective phrases requires a deeper understanding of buyer behavior and search trends.
