# 5 Metadata Mistakes Killing Your Stock Footage Sales

In the competitive world of stock footage, having high-quality clips is just the first step to success. One of the most critical aspects that can make or break your sales is how well you manage your metadata. Metadata acts as the bridge between your footage and potential buyers. If your clips are not selling, it might be due to some common metadata mistakes that you can easily fix.

## Why Metadata Matters for Stock Footage

Metadata is the descriptive information that makes your footage searchable on stock platforms. It includes titles, descriptions, and keywords. When optimized correctly, metadata ensures your clips appear in search results, making them accessible to potential buyers. However, if there's an error or oversight in your metadata, your clips might remain invisible no matter how high-quality they are.

### Top 5 Metadata Mistakes in Stock Footage

Below, we discuss the top five metadata mistakes that could be preventing your stock footage from selling and how you can fix them.

## 1. Poorly Chosen Titles

Your footage title is the first piece of information that potential buyers see. A poorly chosen title can result in your clip being overlooked. 

- **Mistake**: Using vague or generic titles that do not accurately describe your footage.
- **Solution**: Make your titles specific and descriptive. Include relevant keywords that clearly convey the content and context of your footage. For instance, instead of "Sunset", use "Time-lapse of Vibrant Sunset Over Ocean Waves".

## 2. Lack of Relevant Keywords

Keywords are crucial for searchability. Without the right keywords, your footage might not appear in relevant search results.

- **Mistake**: Using too few, irrelevant, or overly broad keywords.
- **Solution**: Conduct keyword research to identify terms that buyers are likely to use. Use specific, relevant keywords that match the content of your footage. Tools like ClipMeta can help you generate effective keywords by analyzing trends and search behaviors.

## 3. Inadequate Descriptions

The description provides context to your footage. An inadequate description might fail to capture what makes your footage unique or useful.

- **Mistake**: Leaving descriptions blank or writing generic descriptions that do not provide additional context.
- **Solution**: Write detailed descriptions that expand on the title. Highlight unique elements of the footage and suggest potential uses. This not only aids in searchability but also helps potential buyers envision how to use your footage.

## 4. Ignoring Metadata Consistency

Consistency in metadata ensures that your clips are easily cataloged and found across platforms. Inconsistencies can lead to confusion and diminished visibility.

- **Mistake**: Using different metadata styles across your collection, leading to inconsistencies.
- **Solution**: Develop a consistent metadata strategy. Use the same format and style across all your clips for titles, descriptions, and keywords. ClipMeta can assist in maintaining consistency across your entire portfolio.

## 5. Failing to Update Metadata

Market trends and search behaviors change over time. Failing to update your metadata means missing out on new opportunities.

- **Mistake**: Not revisiting and updating your metadata to align with current trends and buyer needs.
- **Solution**: Regularly review and update your metadata. Keep an eye on market trends and adjust keywords and descriptions accordingly. This proactive approach keeps your footage relevant and discoverable.

## Enhancing Your Metadata Strategy

Improving your metadata is not a one-time task but an ongoing process. Here are some tips to refine your strategy:

- **Regular Audits**: Periodically review your metadata for accuracy and relevance.
